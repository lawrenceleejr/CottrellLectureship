<p class="lead dropcap">A research university and the small college a half-hour down the road can go
years without sharing a speaker. The <strong>Cottrell Lectureship Series</strong> exists to close
that gap — one teacher-scholar, two talks, one short trip.</p>

## The idea

A [Cottrell Scholar](https://rescorp.org/cottrell-scholars) and a regional partner team up to bring
a visiting teacher-scholar to their corner of the map. On a single trip the visitor speaks twice —
once at each host's institution — so exceptional science and teaching reach both campuses at once,
and two departments that might never have crossed paths start a conversation.

<p class="pullquote">Two talks, two audiences, and two departments that keep talking long after the visitor has flown home.</p>

Each entry on this site is one such visit: the speaker appears in two linked boxes — the teal talk
hosted by the Cottrell Scholar, the terracotta one by the regional partner — each with its own date,
room, and local host.

## Who funds it

The series is supported by the **Research Corporation for Science Advancement (RCSA)** — one of the
oldest U.S. foundations devoted to basic research — as **a Cottrell Collaborative project**, proposed
and led by Cottrell Scholars themselves. The Cottrell Scholar Award recognizes early-career faculty in
chemistry, physics, and astronomy who are as good in the lecture hall as they are in the lab.

## How a pairing works

1. **A pairing forms.** A Cottrell Scholar and a regional partner — at a nearby college or university —
   agree to co-host a visit and settle on the teacher-scholar they'd like to bring.
2. **They apply.** The pair sends a short application to the Cottrell Collaborative describing the visit
   and the speaker.
3. **Two talks, one trip.** The teacher-scholar comes for a single visit and gives a talk at each host's
   institution, reaching two audiences in the same region.
